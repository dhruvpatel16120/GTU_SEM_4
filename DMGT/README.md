## 🛠️ Tutorial Topics

Key topics covered in the tutorials:

1. Propositional logic: Truth values, truth tables & tautology.
2. Predicate logic evaluation over finite domain.
3. Set operations and verifying identities.
4. Counting: nPr, nCr, Pascal triangle & Pigeonhole Principle.
5. Recurrence relations: iteration & closed-form.
6. Functions: mapping, composition & inverse.
7. Permutations & group basics: cycle notation, subgroup generation.
8. Normal Subgroup, Lagrange Theorem, Rings and Field.
9. Relations: Properties and examples.
10. Transitive closure: Warshall algorithm & reachability matrix.
11. Partial orders & Hasse diagram generation.
12. Lattice: Distributive, Modular & complemented.
13. Graph basics: Adjacency matrix, degree, connected components.
14. Graph algorithms: BFS/DFS, shortest path, cycle detection.
15. Weighted graphs: Dijkstra & MST (Kruskal) + tree traversals.

### 💡 Pro-Tips for High Marks:

<p>💡TIP</p>

> **Use the Practice Sets!** The problems in `practice set/` folder frequently appear in exams with minor variations.

- 📌 **Truth Tables:** Use proper logic notation (e.g., ∨, ∧, →) and double-check your truth values.
- 📌 **Hasse Diagrams:** Practice minimal/maximal elements carefully; don't miss the direction in diagrams.
- 📌 **Warshall’s Algorithm:** Learn the flowchart first, then step-by-step logic.
- 📌 **Recurrence relations:** iteration & closed-form.
- 📌 **Functions:** mapping, composition & inverse.
- 📌 **Graph Isomorphism:** Practice minimal/maximal elements carefully; don't miss the direction in diagrams.
- 📌 **Adjacency & Incidence Matrices:** Practice minimal/maximal elements carefully; don't miss the direction in diagrams
- 📌 **Graph Algorithms:** kruskal's algorithm, prim's algorithm, bfs, dfs
- 📌 **Tree Traversals:** Binary tree questions on Pre-order, In-order, and Post-order are always high-scoring areas.
